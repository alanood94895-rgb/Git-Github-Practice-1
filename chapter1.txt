The map was not meant to be found—at least, that’s what Elin believed the moment her fingers brushed away the dust. Tucked beneath a loose floorboard in her family’s creaking cottage, it seemed older than memory itself, its ink shimmering faintly as though alive. Strange symbols curled across its surface, shifting when she looked too closely, and at its center lay a forest she had never seen, yet somehow recognized. The villagers always warned of the woods beyond the hills, where whispers carried on the wind. Now, with the map trembling in her hands, Elin wondered if those whispers had been calling her all along.
